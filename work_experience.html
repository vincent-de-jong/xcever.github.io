The implementation of visible-light photocrosslinkers into Lipoaspirate for autologous reconstructive surgery

The PhD project revolves around the use of visible-light photocrosslinkers (Ruthenium and Sodium Persulfate) in the use of stabilizing patient-derived Lipoaspirates used in reconstructive surgical procedures.
The research focuses on standardizing procedures that enable more predictable crosslinking results, aiming to compensate for large patient to patient variablity.

During the PhD, Vincent has worked closely with plastic reconstructive surgeons to ensure that the developments in the lab remain transferable into the clinic.

Developing the Lab-scale production plant for In-air microparticle fabrication

I rejoined IamFluidics after my original plans to travel were scraped during Lockdown. Before I left, I had transferred my knowledge to a collegue and was now taking on a new role in the company, developing the first lab-scale production facility for IamFluidics.
My responsibilities ranged over several different sections.
The facility needed to comply with cGMP, which meant that all the equipment we developed, as well as logistical work-flows, storage and quality control processes needed to be designed in compliance. I was responsible for matching these requirements to the allocated space.
Additionally, I was still consulting on the pilot-scale fabrication machine and leading the development of the CIP machine that would be used for all required equipment. (with the help of an external consulting firm).

In July of that year we began training my replacement, transfering knowledge and skills, before leaving at the end of August to pursue a postgraduate degree in Bioengineering at the University of Oregon, USA.

During this time, I learned a lot about developing processes, and spent a lot of time in meetings with external parties, further learning how to manage and lead my own projects.
